Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Factoreo and its internal publishing tool, Factoreo Uploader ("the app"), handle information. Factoreo is a personal faceless YouTube Shorts channel. The app is used solely by the channel owner.

What the app does

Factoreo Uploader is a command-line tool the channel owner runs on their own computer to upload and schedule the owner's own videos to the owner's own YouTube channel via the YouTube Data API v3. It does not serve other users and has no public sign-up.

Information we access

When the owner authorizes the app, Google grants it access to the owner's YouTube account using these scopes:

  • youtube.upload — to upload video files to the owner's channel.
  • youtube — to set each video's title, description, tags, category, audience setting and publish schedule.

The app only reads and writes data for the owner's own channel. It does not access any other person's Google or YouTube data.

How information is stored and used

  • OAuth access and refresh tokens are stored locally on the owner's computer (in a token.json file) and are used only to call the YouTube Data API on the owner's behalf.
  • The app does not transmit tokens or channel data to any third-party server, and it does not collect analytics or personal data from any visitor.
  • Video titles, descriptions and files come from the owner's own local project folder.

Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties. No advertising or tracking is performed by the app.

Limited Use disclosure

Factoreo Uploader's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. Data obtained through the YouTube Data API is used only to provide the app's single function — publishing the owner's videos to the owner's channel — and for no other purpose.

Data retention and deletion

Tokens remain on the owner's computer until the owner deletes the local token.json file or revokes access at Google Account → Third-party access. Revoking access immediately ends the app's ability to reach the account.
